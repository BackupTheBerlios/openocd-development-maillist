On Fri, Nov 6, 2009 at 10:02 AM, David Brownell <david-b at pacbell.net> wrote:
>> trailing 0 => we have yet to cut a bugfix release as we intend
>> to have short cycles.
> (...)
> Note that U-Boot is used in embedded development, and
> they chose the "2009.11" style numbering on grounds
> that it was better than the "x.y.z" numbering.  It's
> a lot more meaningful to people ... it's easy to
> remember "issue X resolved late last year" and thus
> to know that a "2009.x" release has it resolved.

David, it could be also nice to include the release date in the
release number (ie. 2009.11.6, or simply 2009.x as you propose) - this
scheme is most excessive in information, I guess, and people may
better remember the date than the number. Also if the "short cycles"
are planned, there could be more than one (bugfix?) release made in
one month - so the x number will simply increase, or the whole release
date will show up in the release number. This approach also looks
tempting. However this scheme somehow looses the project progress
(1.4.20 is the first stable release with 4 new features and 20
bigfixes).
