On Saturday 14 November 2009, Michel Catudal wrote:
>  There are some low cost AVR32 and ARM boards allready made that can run embedded Linux.
>  
>  http://shop.embedded-projects.net/product_info.php/language/en/amp;ref/2/info/p12
>  The grasshopper is only €85, the atmel board is around $89
>  http://www.atmel.com/dyn/products/tools_card.asp?tool_id=4102

The SPI controller driver on those boards is fairly robust,
and those boards give high speed connectivity over either
USB or Ethernet.  Looks like the grasshopper can be powered
over USB ... the NGW isn't wired that way.  (Neither will do
power-over-Ethernet.)

You could do direct JTAG interface to the SPI ports, if
a 3.3V connector was OK.


>  The beagle board is a bit more at $149
>  http://beagleboard.org/

No Ethernet included; but this can be USB powered.  :)

I/O on this is at 1.8V, so you'll need level shifters for
most purposes.  Or a CPLD.

This one will let you run OpenGL animations of your
JTAG operations, should you feel motivated ...


>  It should be easy to port openocd to one of those.

There are LOTS of other ARM boards too; look around.
The ones Michel mentioned have significant followings
on Linux though; they're very hacker-accessible.
