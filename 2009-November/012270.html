> I'm interested in adding support for the Bus Pirate to OpenOCD

> source serial interface tool with an emulated serial port (FTDI chip).
> It previously had an integrated XSVF JTAG programmer, but it was
> difficult to use.
> 
> Usually I like to emulate existing hardware, but I didn't see any serial
> port devices listed in openocd.texi. Is there any reason I shouldn't
> even attempt to this?

You can't emulate something without ... something to emulate!  :)


> The other option is to add support to OpenOCD. Looking at the source, I
> think it would be easiest to modify one of the FTDI bitbang programmers
> to use the Bus Pirate binary mode command format over a serial
> connection. Does that sound reasonable?

Don't touch the ft2232 code; just write a new interface driver.

From what I've skimmed of your design (USB-UART to PIC to <whatever>),
it's not got much in common with other JTAG interfaces in the tree.
So you will end up writing all new code, anyway.
