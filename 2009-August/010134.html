>> So someone should start writing those documentation, so that it can be

> Yep; though I think such "builder docs" should not be
> part of the "user's guide" (openocd.texi) or of the
> "developer's manual" (doxygen stuff).
>   
Why not?

Any general info a user needs to do to use OpenOCD belongs in the 
manual, IMHO - maybe a separate chapter or appendix for each operating 
system that needs special considerations.

Now distribution-specific things are probably best left to the 
distributors, but I think there are enough common things that do not 
depend on the distribution, but on common factors (like libftdi, .in 
files, drivers ...) that warrant inclusion in the manual.
> Windows based distros should eventually avoid a lot of
> that once they sort out the build probs for libftdi...
>   
Yes. However, until then, documenting the necessary steps in one 
easy-to-find place will help users and distributors.
