On Sunday 25 October 2009, Øyvind Harboe wrote:
> How do I add a sign-off line? Do I do this after I've tested
> something that someone else committed or just something
> that I committed myself?

"git am -s" as you merge a patch from someone, "git commit -s"
as you merge your own changes.  But ... be sure to read what it
means, in linux/Documentation/SubmittingPatches section 12.

Strictly speaking "Signed-off-by" is for folk in the patch
creation/merge path ... patch author, subsystem maintainer,
and so on.  Testing success gets a "Tested-by" report.  And
if someone wants to say "yes, good" an "Acked-by" is the way
to go. (Also, cosmetic fixes before commits are OK; they do
not invalidate previous signoffs.)
