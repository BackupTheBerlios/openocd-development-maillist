IANAL, but stating your intentions publicly to violate the GPL license
does not improve your standing in any way...

The contributors are working with SFLC(as posted a while ago)
to get in place the routines to handle this the right way and while
it would be unsavory to solicit evidence of GPL violations we forward
what we are handed to our contact at SFLC so he can work with it.

Lawyers in general keep their cards close to their chest, it's a bit
like playing cards, so if you don't see a lot of activity, doesn't
mean that nothing isn't happening.... ;-)

Big players know that these things can take a long time and have
huge impacts, so they rarely blatantly ignore GPL. Ignorance
does exist in large organizations though...

the details of the violation and I'll give it for our SFLC contact to chew on.
