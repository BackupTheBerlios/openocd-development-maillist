> AFAIK, other reset commands try to use the SRST/TRST which may not be
> there. TRST is clearly redundant, as the same functionality (TAP reset)
> should be obtained by sending at least five 1's to TMS.

That's not accurate. TRST and 5x 1 for TMS are not equivalent. TRST resets
ICE registers.

Also, there are CPUs out there that have some hanky-panky going on w/SRST
and TRST in order to protect data in flash against prying eyes(e.g.
JTAG controller
being disconnected in software), so I'm not convinced that soft_reset_halt is
the canonical way.

> The reason why we still have SRST and TRST is that not all chip
> manufacturers have been reading the standards too well.

I'm not sure that the story is quite that simple. I believe there are some awful
truths behind this cabling that aren't being revealed. Haven't seen a
crisp authorotative
source on the srst+trst+tms reset story across all ARM CPU implementations,
but it sure would be welcome!
