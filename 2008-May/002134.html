For toying around with OpenOCD for hobby purposes(debugging
OpenOCD, messing around with the OpenOCD code, making it work on new
and wonderful targets, etc.), then I quite like the parallel port bitbang
driver. It boils away lot of things that could get in the way of a crisp
understanding of every last detail of what is going on.

Another thing that I've been thinking about for the bitbang OpenOCD
driver would be to have a playback driver.

Basically the bitbang driver could be modified to save *all* input/output
to a file. This file could then be passed to a playback bitbang driver
that allows precise sequences to be debugged by a developer who
has no access to the target. These playback files could be committed
to subversion(they would be *VERY* tied to particular versions of
subversion anyway).
