>> That's not accurate. TRST and 5x 1 for TMS are not equivalent. TRST resets
>> ICE registers.
>
> AFAIK, the JTAG standard talks about resetting the TAP. Then after the TAP
> is in a known state, it can be used to configure/read/write/whatever
> everything behind it. I think here the ARM people have not understood the
> JTAG standard, and now we suffer.
>
> I do not really know why the TRST was added to IEEE 1149.1, as it should be
> equivalent to the "five 1's". One reason I've heard is that if the pin is
> grounded, then the TAP is held in reset, and there won't be any ill effects
> from unconnected JTAG pins floating around. However, this is not a good
> reason (as the pins should be properly terminated in any case).
>
> And yes, my post described the things as I think they were originally meant
> to be. The real world is much more cruel than that, but my point of view is
> that if JTAG was implemented the way it should be, we wouldn't need the
> annoying extra lines. But, yes, it isn't and we sometimes need.
>
> - Ville
