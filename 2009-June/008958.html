On Monday 29 June 2009, Joseph Kuss wrote:
> FTDI is a chip company correct ?, I believe,  openOCD  consortium or
> whatever it is you guys call yourself have reasons why they must
> keep to the GPL terms, 

FTDIchip.com can do whatever they want, but folk distributing
OpenOCD binaries must obey the GPL.  And that precludes use
of the D2XX library from FTDI.


> Which you could explain to me if you want, but for end users like me,
> I could on my own take your open source code and  link to FTDI's
> library, and FTDI would not sue me or my company,

Absolutely.  That's the preferred way to use FTDI's code with
GPL-licensed software:  build it yourself.  If you're using
MS-Windows, that's your best solution for using OpenOCD at
the moment.


> as long  as I am not selling some kind of debugger that has 
> their code in it. Is this correct ?? It is not like the library
> is pirated or somthing, right ?

FTDI doesn't much care what you do with their library.
They make it available for free ("Free as in Beer").


> Perhaps I could find the latest FTDI library on thier web site ?

In fact that should be *very* easy to find, they want you
to do that.  It's just that their distribution terms are
not compatible with GPL, so the two chunks of software can't
be distributed together.

No worries if you just want to build a personal copy of the
OpenOCD software combined with the FTDI library.  Just Do It.
