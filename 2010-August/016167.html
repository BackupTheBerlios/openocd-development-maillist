On Mon, Aug 2, 2010 at 10:43 PM, David Brownell <david-b at pacbell.net> wrote:
>> Does anyone have any idea why the
>> lpc1768 requires
>> such a low clock rate?
>>
>> "reset run" seems to require as low as 100kHz.... Could
>> this
>> be due to the app I'm running is messing with the clock?
>
> Probably.  The chip can run at 100 MHz ISTR,
> but if it's running at a slower rate, JTAG will
> of course not be fast either.
>
> reset init methods could jack up the clock to
> more than the current slow default, too...

As near as I can tell RTCK is 4MHz and the config
script says 4MHz / 6 = 666kHz. I.e. RTCK is *not* the
correct jtag frequency... (Experiments confirm that
666kHz seems to be the correct frequency to use)

666kHz works fine for flashing, but it is the "reset run"
that fails. Flashing requires "reset init", i.e. "reset halt".

I'm wondering why "reset run" fails w/the same frequency
that works fine for "reset halt". I've tried with a blank flash
as well as a flash that (I believe) is programmed with a blinky
app.
