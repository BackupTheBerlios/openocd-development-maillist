I'm very happy with Michael taking care of commits. I hoped to find some more time during my christmas holidays, but that was only wishful thinking.

That said, I would still like to keep control over massive changes, but in
this particular case I already acknowledged the basic idea of the change, so I'm fine with it. Michael told me off-list that he had tested the patches, and I'm confident that his testing is better than my testing.

What I'd like to avoid is including patches that favor some commercial
interest over the interests of the majority of OpenOCD users (Wiggler +
FT2232 using only telnet or a real GDB) - like patches that are required to make the OpenOCD work (better) on Vendor X's hardware, while causing problems (performance degradation, loss of functionality) for others, or patches that affect the GDB remote protocol because of Vendor Y's frontend. Øyvind convinced me that this isn't the case with the synthesize... patch.

So no - just being active doesn't give ultimate say over what patches get
included, but at the moment it's probably better to just include it anyway. If we (myself, or anyone else) find something we don't like (for a reason) we can roll the changes back quite easily.

With more committers and less time on my side it might of course be necessary to create some commit rules, feel free to suggest something.

Best regards,

Dominic
