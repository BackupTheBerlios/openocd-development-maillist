I try the latest OpenOCD crosscompiled for Windows with the most recent 
libftdi. With LPC2103 everything works fine, but with STM32 the first 
gdb "load" after power-up of STM32 fails. OpenOCD -d3 log attached 
(first.log [zipped]). The second (and next) try (without shutting down 
OpenOCD - just shutting and restarting gdb) goes fine (second.log 
[zipped] - the log is from a new OpenOCD session, to show the differences).

When I completely disconnect the JTAG, but STM32 remains powered - 
everything works fine, so that's not the issue with JTAG power-up. If I 
leave the JTAG powered, but completely disconnect STM32 - first "load" 
fails.

This affects only "load" via gdb, as I can flash a just-powered-up STM32 
with telnet and "flash write_image ..." without any problems.

In 0.2.0 (mingw32 compilation, libftdi-0.16) it works fine.
In 0.3.0-rc0 (mingw32 compilation, libftdi-0.16) it doesn't.
In 0.3.1 (mingw32 compilation, libftdi-0.16) it doesn't.
In 0.4.0-rc1-dev (i686-w64-mingw32 crosscompilation, libftdi-0.17) it 
doesn't.
In 0.4.0-rc1-dev (i686-w64-mingw32 crosscompilation, ftd2xx) it doesn't.

Any ideas? I'll try bisecting that, but this can take me a lot of time );
