Here are four patches which largely finish the work of making
sure we have a "top level" for ARM cores ... at least, as much
as can be finished for the 0.4.0 release.  The microcontroller
profile cores (Cortex-M0/M1/M3, Thumb2-only and without all
those CPU modes) won't fit until register access is cleaner.

 - Remove mrc_opcode() ... use the MRC() and MCR() macros,
   as appropriate.  This is cleanup plus a bugfix.

 - Move opcode macros to <target/arm_opcodes.h> ... and fix
   some minor related goofage.

 - Rename a few more routines ... armv4_5_*() names are not
   right for things that work just fine on ARMv6 and ARMv7.

 - Move most "armv4_5.h" declarations to "arm.h" ... since
   they're both generic, and generically named.

The "armv4_5.h" file remains, but it's just holding stuff
that complicates support for microcontroller cores, and thus
should get removed.

Barring objections, I'll merge this later today; this change
has been coming for a while, I don't think anyone should be
surprised by it.

- Dave
