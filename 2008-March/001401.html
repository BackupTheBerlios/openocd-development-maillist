The idea here was to split the reset_config into configuration
which regards to the target and reset config which regards
to the capabilities of the interface.

However, perhaps we should just define, for the purposes
of the target library, that all interfaces *must* be capable
of asserting SRST and TRST seperately to be compatible
with the target library?

If the user has a device that is not compatible with that
assumption, then he, manually, has to override
the reset_config?  E.g:

openocd -f target/xxx.cfg -c "reset_config yyy"

>  IMHO, the JTAG driver shall not be more smart than necessary, let someone who knows more decide,
>  that is, the *user* shall make the final decision if (s)he likes.

"someone who knows more", eh? :-) I don't think that would be
the user. I sure get confused by all this reset stuff and would be
eternally gratefull to live in ignorant bliss and leave it to the target
library! If the reset lines do not work, then I want to be able to
control everything and get down to nitty gritties.
