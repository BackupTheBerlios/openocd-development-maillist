On Sunday 23 March 2008 11:26:04 Øyvind Harboe wrote:
> bitbang_interface->read() is invoked sometime after clk
> goes 1.
>
> What does this mean in hardware terms?
>
> Is bitbang_interface->read() supposed to be sampled on
> the rising edge?

TDO should be sampled on the rising edge, and will change on the falling edge. 
Because there is no way to read the signal exactly at the rising edge I 
decided to read after the rising edge.

> Hardware does not have a concept of reading
> bitbang_interface->read() "sometime after clk => 1",
> and conversely sw does not have a concept of reading
> something on the rising edge.
>
> I would like to have a clearifying comment in bitbang.c
> on this point as I'm pushing for bitbang.c to have
> a documentation role.

This is plain IEEE 1149 JTAG - nothing specific to the OpenOCD or its JTAG 
API. Feel free to add a comment, but I doubt many people would look at the 
bitbang code to learn about JTAG basics - there's enough documentation on the 
internet.

Dominic
