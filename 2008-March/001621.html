It looks like a hardware communication problem.

Check your cables, try a different JTAG interface adapter,
lower JTAG clk, put the JTAG signals up on a scope, etc.

>  jtag 130 # openocd --file arm9_ft2232.cfg --search `pwd`
>  Open On-Chip Debugger 1.0 (2008-03-14-20:17) svn:516
>  $URL: svn://svn.berlios.de/openocd/trunk/src/openocd.c $
>  Info:    jtag.c:1329 jtag_examine_chain(): JTAG device found:
>  0xfe0448e3 (Manufacturer: 0x471, Part: 0xe044, Version: 0xf)

I don't know if the above is correct.

Subsequently 0x000000ff should be returned if there are no
other devices on the JTAG chain.

>  Info:    jtag.c:1329 jtag_examine_chain(): JTAG device found:
>  0x00000003 (Manufacturer: 0x001, Part: 0x0000, Version: 0x0)

Hmm... looks like noise.

>  Info:    jtag.c:1329 jtag_examine_chain(): JTAG device found:
>  0x0000007f (Manufacturer: 0x03f, Part: 0x0000, Version: 0x0)


Here I would have expected 0xff, i.e. end of chain. A bit is
missing.
