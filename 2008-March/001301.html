Sorry I didn't reply ealier to this, but it has been a busy week at work, and 
the next one isn't going to be easier. I don't have much time for this now 
either, but before you're going further in this direction I'll like to 
express my concerns:

I don't think I like this change. It's exactly the opposite of how the JTAG 
layer was intended, because you're dropping all context from the scans.

It also increases overhead for all but your implementation:

Instead of one jtag_command_t, there are three in the case of an embedded ice 
write. And instead of one scan of 38 bits length there will be three scans, 
including one of only one bit size. This has a negative impact on the FT2232, 
which is by far the most popular JTAG interface at present.

I hope to find more time to look into this later this evening or tomorrow 
morning.
