Hello Øyvind,

I agree, that the it is hard to understand reset_config, however...

Øyvind Harboe napsal(a):
> right. There are even impossible combinations like
> "reset_config srst_and_trst srst_pulls_trst".

This combination seems perfectly ok to me.

> - if there are unworkable combinations, then the user must receive
>  actionable error messages

> - the interface has limitations w.r.t. what types of reset configurations
>   it can support. This knowledge should be encoded in the driver.

Bad, bad, BAD (really bad!!!) idea, totally broken. The JTAG interface provides
just signals, it ha *no clue* about how these signals may be wired to the target
or how they are actually connected on the target PCB.
The only think the JTAG driver may provide is whether it provides both reset signal
or just a single reset signal (without defining it's meaning, this actually depends
on how the user connects it).

> - the specific target has rules about how a reset must take place. This should
>   be encoded in the target script.

"May take place", not "must take place", if ever, there may be several options.

> - the class of targets(e.g. XScale) has limitations of what sort of
>   reset configurations are possible. XScale *must* have a srst and
>   trst hooked up correctly.

Actually, not only XScale...
I agree there is work to be done on this, however, deprecating reset_config the way you suggested
brings to many limitations I believe...
