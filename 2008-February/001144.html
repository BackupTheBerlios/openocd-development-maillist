Dominic Rath napsal(a):
> Please note that a flash driver doesn't necessarily have a target_t associated.

Good point Dominic!

> Currently the only example is the str9xpec (though I don't know how this one is exactly implemented). The str912 offers two modes to program the on-chip 
> flash - either using the CPU, or directly via a separate TAP interface. Maybe Spen can comment on the sideeffects this has, and how useable the target_t is 
> when the str912 is accessed via str9xpec.
> 
> It's also possible to have flash drivers that have absolutely no target associated, for example flash that's connected to a FPGA, and which is programmed by 
> bit-banging the FPGA's boundary scan interface, or flash that has its own JTAG port (Xilinx comes to my mind). This isn't currently implemented, by I see no 
> reason why the interface shouldn't support such flashes.

Ok, that is a strong argument against the flash wrappers as they are in the trunk - restricting flash driver
API too much in what it can do. And that is exactly what I would like to change.
