We're using a semi-formal procedure (very similar to what Linux kernel
developers do) in the coreboot project (www.coreboot.org):

 - Every patch must be sent to the list, with a line such as

   Signed-off-by: Uwe Hermann <uwe at hermann-uwe.de>

 - The patch gets reviewed by other developers, and can only be
   committed after at least one developer formally ACKs it via:

   Acked-by: John Doe <foo at bar.com>

This ensures that at least two developers looked at the code and think
it's ok. After a bit of time it took to get familiar with this system it
now works really, really well.
