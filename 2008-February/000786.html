> How about making halt synchronous with an optional timeout
> argument?
> 
> Any objections?

That's just what "halt", followed by a "wait_halt" does.

In any case, you wont be able to remove all asynchronous behaviour, because target events will always be asynchronous. We wont get rid of wait_halt, because that covers the case where you're waiting for a breakpoint to hit.

Having halt asynchronous allows you to issue halt to multiple targets almost "at once", waiting for them to enter debug one after the other.

IMHO we'd loose some flexibility for little gain, other than saving one line per halt in a script.
