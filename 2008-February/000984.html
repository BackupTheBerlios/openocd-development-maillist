It's not like write_binary uses some obscure black magic - one linear code path, that calls directly into the driver's write routine. I think the risk of bit-rot is rather small here...

> Also, if Dominic is the only one who speaks up for addressing flash
> by sector(a hellish proposition when the sector sizes vary in size! :-),
> perhaps we should have another way to translate from sector to
> address that has less "surface area" towards the great masses.
> 
> Perhaps we could define a sector prefix and add a fn to parse an
> address or sector?
> 
> flash erase_address *0 0x8000
> 
> - Here "*" is used to indicate that a sector is specified instead
>   of an address.
> - Length is probably fine as is, no?
> - rename "flash erase_address" to "flash erase".

Sad as this may be, flash is based on sectors, which can only be earsed as a whole. Erasing based on a bank and a range of sectors that should be erased (either by first and last, or by first and length) is the most natural interface to this concept.
