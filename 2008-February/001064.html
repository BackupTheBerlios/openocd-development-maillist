Hi,

maybe you could just adopt the Linux kernel coding style, see

  http://lxr.linux.no/linux/Documentation/CodingStyle

It's pretty similar to the guidelines you lined out and it's a pretty
well-known and wide-spread coding style. Also, you can automatically
adjust most code to the guidelines using the 'indent' tool:

  $ indent -npro -kr -i8 -ts8 -sob -l80 -ss -ncs *.[ch]

(I would not entirely trust 'indent', sometimes the results need small
manual fixes, but usually it's pretty good).
