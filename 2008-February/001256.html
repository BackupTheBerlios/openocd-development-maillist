The reason for do ... while(0) in macro definitions is almost the same as for 
parens in definitions like
 
#define y(x) (1+x) 
rather than
#define y(x) 1+x 
although the last definition also will compile and act correctly in many 
contexts.

do...while is at least used for multi statement definitions like

#define y(x) do {x++; y= b;} while(0)
rather than
#define y(x) x++; y= 1;

The difference becomes obvious in this setting:

if (z < 0) y(x);

which in both cases will compile, but not yield the same results

do...while also allows for local variables within the block
