Doesn't sound like you have choice but committing. Unless there
someone who can test this reasonably quickly(a day or two).

We have *only* a dozen targets at our office. Realistically it's going to
take a while before *all* combinations are retested as nobody will have
even a fraction of the interface/target combinations available.

Obviously we need to commit meanwhile lest the rountrip times would
become fantastically long, patches would have to be updated
and OpenOCD would not progress.

This is why the testing/index.html document distinguishes between
testing of released code and testing before committing.

We have subversion and if your patch, which you've done a fair
bit of smoketesting on, breaks some target, then we have to
rely on subversion to go back and compare.
