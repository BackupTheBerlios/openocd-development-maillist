I have just installed OpenOCD in Ubuntu with ftdilib support. 
Everything else goes rather fine, but when I try to move larger
blocks with arm7_9 fast_memory_access enabled, the FTDI driver
dies.

I know this is a known problem. I played around for some time
with the FT2232_BUFFER_SIZE. With 1024 it works, with 2048 it
does not. The speed is somewhat disappointing with that setting.

This issue has something to do with ftdilib not being similar to
D2XX, which again has a lot to do with FTDIs stupid policy of not
telling anything about the interface. But as I would like to
see this sorted, I'd like to know what is behind this.

So, what do we know about this problem?

- what does the limit depend on? System speed? USB hardware? Any
guesses?

- what limits have been demonstrated to work?

- is there some other reason to the slow performance of ftdilib
than this?

- any guesses why things go dramatically wrong?

- any way to recover from the error? (i.e. do we know how much
data we've lost?)


I am using the newest version (0.12) of ftdilib, if it matters.

- Ville
