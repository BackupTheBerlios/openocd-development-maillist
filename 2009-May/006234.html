On Friday 08 May 2009, Anders Montonen wrote:
> If I remember the USB spec correctly, the lowest achievable round-trip  
> time (ie. one OUT transfer followed by one IN transfer) is two  
> milliseconds for low- and full-speed devices. 

Depends on the implementation of the host controller driver
and silicon errata.

ISTR that OHCI on Linux was once two msec *per transfer* but
had to add another msec to cope with some buggy silicon.  So
that realistically is closer to 5 msec, maybe 6 msec.  UHCI
doesn't have that annoying erratum (not many silicon vendors
to commit such goofage!), so it's a bit quicker.

And EHCI -- full speed through a highspeed hub -- can be much
quicker than that.  In theory, that round-trip could take less
than one millisecond.


But yes, the conclusion that USB should avoid synchronous
calls is very accurate.
